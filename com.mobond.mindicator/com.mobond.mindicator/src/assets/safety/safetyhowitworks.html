Women Safety

What are the different features provided in this section?

1. Send Panic SMS to Railways and relative

2. Complain through Call, WhatsApp to railways

3. Send automatic location SMS to relative after 2 consecutive missed calls between 9 pm and 8 am


How Unique is it?

  • It doesn't require GPS or internet.
  • Keeping in mind that in most of the emergency cases, victim is unable to access the phone, automatic location alert SMS facility is provided.


How "Panic SMS" feature works?

  1. In m-Indicator local train section, you can set train.
  2. Select coach position in train(FRONT, MIDDLE or END) and destination of train.
  3. Press "Save" button & exit the application.
  4. Click on the "Security" icon.
  5. Press Panic SMS button, location SMS is immediately sent to your relative and RPF


How “Automatic Emergency SMS” feature works?

Women in Mumbai are mainly unsecured during night times after 9 pm. Keeping this thing in mind, from 9 pm to 8 am if you receive 2 missed calls from the person whose number is registered in m-Indicator, it sends an automatic SMS to that person about her approximate location.


How “Automatic SMS” feature is secure?

  • This feature works from evening 9 pm to morning 8 am i.e. which are generally non-working hours.
  • SMS is sent only to the registered relative. No other person shall receive these SMS.
  • When SMS is automatically sent by your phone it shows pop up message on your screen that automatic SMS has been sent to your relative. This will remind you about Safety Settings' registered contact numbers.

Women Safety Instructions

  1. Avoid all travels at night. Never get into a cab or auto that already occupied with any male passenger.
  2. Your eye contact with stranges should be brief and never intimating.
  3. Act confident and try not making enquiries from strangers.
  4. Keep some speed-dial numbers and some self-defence devices handy.
  5. If the ladies compartment is empty, board the general one instead. It is always better to be in the company of a group of people (men or women) rather than none at all.
  6. Carry a pen knife or pepper spray for self-defense. A torch is handy if the lights go off.
  7. If travelling late, try and board the compartment very next to the driver’s coach.
  8. Avoid late night travelling to suburbs when you are alone. If you have to, take someone along.
  9. Don’t stand or hang near the door. If the train is over-crowded, skip it and take the next one.
  10. Wearing expensive jewellery in the train is a big NO. Keep them in your handbag if you have to.
  11. Be extremely watchful about the people who board the train. If you notice anyone suspicious, try and attract the attention of someone on the platform.
  12. Don’t ignore, Raise your Voice calmly, firmly, and without insults or personal attacks to let them know that their actions are unwelcome, unacceptable, and wrong.
  13. Do not go into arguments/shouting- what are you doing? Or why you’re doing this to me, instead, ‘Name the Act’ ex: ‘You (He) pinched me’. ‘Why are you staring at me this way?’.
  14. Scream, be confident and be loud enough when you confront the harasser.
  15. Don’t hesitate to take help of bystanders.
  16. Call Mumbai Police: 103

Role of Bystanders

  1. As a bystander, try to intervene in the matter where women might require help. In such situation yell, scream and draw more public attention.
  2. If you come across any situation of violence on women, engage the victim in some way that will distract from harasser. Ex: ‘Can you tell me the time?’ ‘I need to go there, which train I must take?’ .
  3. Call on Helpline numbers and report about the harassment that you witnessed.
